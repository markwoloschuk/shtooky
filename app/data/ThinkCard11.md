One way to think about it: Bad complexity → confusion. Good complexity → depth. Bad simplicity → oversimplification. Good simplicity → clarity. Design lives in the space between those. Early in a career, designers often think the job is: make things simpler. Later, many realize the job is actually: make things understandable. Those are not the same thing. Sometimes understanding requires compression. Sometimes it requires layering. Your statement also hints at something philosophical. You said: “Good design will express itself.” That suggests the designer is discovering something rather than inventing it. Many great designers describe the process that way.
