[paragraph]
A sales conference video. Five subjects, multiple topics, a noisy convention floor. The brief called for a loop. Mark called for something else.

[pullquote]
The question wasn't how. <It was whether.>

[paragraph]
An extended loop on a convention floor is a video nobody watches. The question wasn't how to make a better loop — it was whether a loop was the right format at all.[br]Redesigned the delivery mechanism before shooting a single frame. Bite-sized segments organized by topic. Interactive UI. Quiz with prizes. Interrotron interviews — the Errol Morris technique — for genuine eye contact. Built in Flash. It worked.[br]The most important creative decision on this project happened before we shot a single frame. The question isn't always how to make it — sometimes it's whether to make it at all.
