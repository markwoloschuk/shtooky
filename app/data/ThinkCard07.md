---
title: Iterate but don't thrash
narrowtitle: Iterate but[br]don't thrash
subtitle: Patience is a creative skill, so is knowing when to move on.
imagePath: /images/think/07/
---

[paragraph]
Iteration is how good work gets made. Nobody ships the first version of a real idea. The first version is a stake in the ground — a declaration of intent that the work then spends time trying to live up to. Every round of feedback, every refinement, every moment where you look at what you made and decide it's not there yet — that's the job. Iteration isn't failure. It's the process.

[paragraph]
But iteration without convergence is thrashing. There's a version of it that looks like diligence but is actually drift — you keep changing things, but not getting closer to anything. The work gets different without getting better. You're still moving, but the movement has stopped having direction. The skill is recognizing the difference. Productive iteration has a feeling of closing in on something. Thrashing has the feeling of circling.

[pullquote]
More rounds doesn't mean[br]
better work. It means more <decisions>.

[paragraph]
I've been caught in both. Thrashing usually happens when I've lost confidence in the direction before I've given it a fair shot, or when I'm adding feedback without synthesizing it — treating every note as its own separate problem instead of asking what all the notes together are telling me about the work. The way out isn't more iteration. It's stopping, stepping back, and deciding whether the direction is right before grinding away at the execution.

[paragraph]
Patience and decisiveness aren't opposites in this process — they're the same skill applied at different moments. The patience to stay in a direction long enough to know whether it works. The decisiveness to commit once you can see it's working, or to change direction once you can see it isn't. Both require the same thing: an honest read of where you actually are, without the story you've been telling yourself about where you thought you'd be by now.
