[paragraph]
One of the easiest traps in creative work is confusing your idea with the answer. I've done it myself. You spend hours refining a concept, and before long you're defending it because it's yours—not because it's the best solution anymore. Ideas don't deserve loyalty. Problems do.

[paragraph]
Great collaborators don't arrive with answers they refuse to let go of. They arrive with curiosity. They put ideas on the table, let other people build on them, and aren't afraid to throw away yesterday's favorite if today's insight is better.

[pullquote]
Leggo my ego

[paragraph]
If your favorite idea isn’t serving the goal – kill it. Merge your idea with others. Let someone else’s suggestion win. Steal from yourself if you have to. Ego should never triumph over purpose.

[paragraph]
The problem doesn't care who solves it. Neither should you.
