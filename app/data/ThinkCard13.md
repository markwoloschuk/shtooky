Communication is a key purpose/goal of design. And I think problem solving is definitely another. Those are clearly different things.

But guiding behavior and improving usability sound kind of like the same thing. Producing an emotional response also sounds like a form of communication – I want you to feel a particular way.

Organizing complexity also seems to stand apart – although maybe it too is a form of problem solving. If something is too complex to visualize or understand – then we solve that problem through design by organizing it better (which is also improving usability which is also then maybe problem solving?).

So maybe it's just the two things then? Communication and Problem Solving.

You’re doing something very “designerly” here: compressing categories until the structure reveals itself. That instinct—to reduce complexity to a few governing ideas—is basically the same cognitive move behind good design systems. And you’re right that many of those categories collapse into each other.
