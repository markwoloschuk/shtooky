[paragraph]
When working on video projects, one of the first question I like to ask is “why is this video?”. Not what – why? A video is a choice — expensive, time-consuming, a specific kind of experience for a specific kind of attention. If the answer to “why is this a video” is “because we always do videos” or “because the brief said video,” that’s worth knowing before you shoot a single frame.


[paragraph]
Questions are the greatest design tools – fast, cheap and everyone knows how to use them. Some questions are obvious, and so are some answers. But more often they’re the fuel that feeds the creative fire. A brief isn’t a set of instructions or a recipe – it’s an opening statement to conversation and exploration.

[pullquote]
Splitting infinity


[paragraph]
Design doesn’t start in a box that you escape through creativity. It starts in formless infinity — endless possibility, no direction, no constraints. The brief is how you build the box. Questions, definitions, constraints, decisions — each one a wall that makes the space more workable. A good brief isn’t a limitation. It’s the architecture that makes good decisions possible.


[paragraph]
And good design, once it’s in the world, keeps the conversation going. The best work leaves something unsaid — not because it’s incomplete, it’s so we create a gap where the audience can enter. 


[paragraph]
I’ve designed a lot of t-shirts. Clients often want more text – more context – more explanation. Whenever possible I try to lead with a bold visual statement that invites question and conversation. When people are inspired to ask what a design means I think we deliver a bigger more meaningful message.

[paragraph]
Good design is never a monologue. In the making, it asks questions. In the world, it starts them.
